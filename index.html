What is crowdfunding?

  1. raising money from the mass market, for a project or business
  2. "The practice of funding a project or venture by raising many small amounts of money from a large number of people, typically by the internet"
  3. three types of actors
    1. project initiator - propses the idea
    2. individuals - who support the project
    3. moderation organization - the platform that bring the initiator and individuals together

History of crowdfunding

  1. 1885: Joseph Pullitzer launches fundraising campaign for the Pedestal of the Statue of Liberty
  2. 1997: Marillion (a Rock Band) funds a US tour with online donations
  3. 2005: Kiva launches as micro-lending platform
  4. 2000: ArtistShare (for music) is the first crowdfunding platform
  5. 2006: First record of the word “crowdfunding” (used by Michael Sullivan)
  1. 2009: Kickstarter Launches as platform for companies and campaigns
  2. 2010: GrowVC launches as first equity crowdfunding platform
  3. 2014: Zach Brown tries crowdfunding a potato salad
    1. Goal: 10$
    2. Totally raised: 55'492$

Types of

crowdfunding

  1. Reward-based
    1. investors receive a tangible item or service
    2. invest in film, and get tickets to the premier
    3. special rewards
  1. Donation-based
    1. receiving nothing in return
  1. Equity-based
    1. receive a stake/shares in the company
    2. provision of finance in the form of equity
    3. allows a large number of individuals to invest in a project and receive compensation
    4. most commonly been used to fund startups
  1. Lending-based
    1. investors get the money back they lent over a fixed timespan
